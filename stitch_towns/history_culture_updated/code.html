Our Living History

Exploring the roots, traditions, and stories of Heritage Town since 1824.

The Journey Through Time

Trace our town's evolution from a small settlement to a thriving community.

1824

Foundation

First settlers arrived by the river bank, establishing the central trading post that would become our town square.

1892

The Great Railway

Connectivity blossomed as the East-West railway line connected our local industry to the national market.

1955

Cultural Renaissance

Establishment of the Town Library and the first annual Heritage Festival, now a staple of our culture.

Cultural Traditions

The Autumn Harvest Gala

A two-century-old tradition where local farmers share their bounty. The event is famous for the 'River Dance' and the crowning of the Harvest King.

Artisans of the Valley

Our town's unique blue-glaze pottery has been passed down through seven generations. Every summer, masters teach youth the ancient wheel techniques.
